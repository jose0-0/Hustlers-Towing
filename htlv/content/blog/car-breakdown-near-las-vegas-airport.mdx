The area around Harry Reid International Airport is one of the more complicated places to deal with a vehicle problem in Las Vegas. Koval Lane, Paradise Road, Tropicana Avenue, and the I-15 interchange nearby all carry heavy traffic at almost any hour. The airport approach roads have their own access rules. And if you are in a rental car or picking someone up, the pressure of a schedule makes everything feel more urgent.

Here is a practical guide to handling a breakdown in the airport area, whether you are in your own car or a rental.

## If You Are in a Rental Car

This is the first question to answer because it changes who you call. Rental cars come with their own roadside assistance, and using it is almost always the right first move.

Look in the glove box for the rental agreement and emergency contact card. Every major rental company — Enterprise, Hertz, Avis, Budget, National, Alamo — has a 24-hour roadside line. Call that number first. They will either send a technician, arrange a tow, or in many cases simply swap you into a different vehicle so you can continue on your way without waiting for a repair.

If the rental company's response time is longer than you can manage given your schedule, or if they are not responsive, you can call an independent towing company and then work out the reimbursement with the rental company afterward. Keep all receipts.

## If You Are in Your Own Vehicle

If your car breaks down in the airport area and you are not in a rental, the process is the same as any other Las Vegas breakdown — just with a few location-specific things to know.

The cell phone lot on the south side of the airport is a safe place to pull over if your car is still running and you can make it there. It is a legitimate waiting area, there is space to pull off, and you are not blocking traffic.

The roads immediately surrounding the terminal — the departures and arrivals loops — have zero tolerance for stopped vehicles that are not actively loading or unloading passengers. If your car dies in those loops, you will have airport police on you within minutes. Do not attempt to change a tire or work on the car there. The only option is to get the car moving or call us and get a tow organized as fast as possible.

Koval Lane and Paradise Road are your best bet for finding a safe shoulder or parking lot if you need to stop and make calls before help arrives.

## Getting to the Airport on Time if the Car Cannot Be Fixed Quickly

If you or someone you are picking up has a flight and the car situation cannot be resolved in time, do not try to wait it out. Call a rideshare, call a cab, or ask someone who is with you to handle the airport run while you stay with the vehicle and wait for the tow.

We can take care of the car. Missing a flight because you refused to leave the vehicle is not a trade-off worth making.

Leave the car keys with the tow driver if you need to leave before the truck arrives. Confirm in advance where the vehicle will be taken so you can retrieve it when you are back. We will secure the vehicle and get it to your mechanic or storage location.

## Parking Structure Situations

If your car breaks down in one of the airport parking structures — the main parking garage, the economy lot on Gilespie Street, or the valet areas — there are a few additional considerations.

Multi-level parking structures require a flatbed that can navigate the ramp angles and height restrictions. When you call us, tell us you are in a parking structure, which level you are on, and the approximate height clearance at the entrance so we can confirm the right equipment.

The economy lot on Gilespie is generally more accessible than the main structure. The main terminal parking garage has tighter ramp angles and lower clearance in some sections.

## Common Breakdown Scenarios Near the Airport

Dead battery from sitting in long-term parking for an extended trip is one of the most common calls we get from the airport area. If your car has been sitting in the long-term lot for a week or more in Las Vegas summer heat, the battery has been cooking the whole time. Have jumper cables in the car before you leave for a long trip, and if the car will not start after returning, call us for a jump start.

Locked keys in the car happens frequently in the departure rush when people are moving fast and distracted. Our lockout service covers the airport area around the clock.

Flat tires in the parking lot structures happen more than you would expect given the traffic density and the occasional debris on ramp surfaces. If you have a flat and a functional spare and you are in a safe area of the lot, you can change it yourself. If you are in a tight spot, it is dark, or you do not have a spare, call us.
