You are standing next to your car, staring at your keys sitting on the driver's seat, and your phone is either dead or at 8 percent. It happens to people every single day in Las Vegas. The question is who you call.

Most people's first instinct is to search for a locksmith. But in Las Vegas, a towing company with lockout service is often the faster and more affordable option. Here is an honest breakdown of how the two compare so you can make the right call the next time it happens to you.

## What a Towing Company Lockout Service Does

Towing companies that offer lockout service send a technician who uses specialized tools to open your vehicle without a key. The most common methods are a slim jim, an air wedge combined with a reach tool, or in some cases a lock pick set depending on the vehicle and lock type.

This is a standard service for most towing companies, not a specialty offering. It is typically faster than a traditional locksmith because towing companies already have trucks positioned around the city for breakdown calls. The same dispatcher handling roadside calls is the one sending someone to help you with a lockout.

## What a Locksmith Does

A locksmith is trained specifically in locks and security systems. For a car lockout, they can typically open the door, cut a new key on the spot if needed, or re-key the lock. If your situation involves a lost key rather than just locked keys inside the car, a locksmith is the right call because they can replace the key entirely.

For a standard locked-keys-in-the-car situation, a locksmith does the same job as a towing company lockout service, just sometimes slower and often more expensive.

## Response Time in Las Vegas

This is where towing companies tend to win. A towing company with multiple trucks spread around the Las Vegas valley typically has someone closer to you than a standalone locksmith shop would.

Las Vegas is a large city and locksmith businesses are not evenly distributed. If you are in Henderson, near the airport, or in the northwest valley, the nearest available locksmith may be significantly farther away than the nearest available tow truck.

Towing companies also operate 24/7 as a baseline since roadside calls happen around the clock. Locksmiths vary — some offer 24-hour service and some do not, and after-hours locksmith calls often carry premium pricing.

## Cost Comparison

For a basic locked-keys-in-the-car situation, towing company lockout service is generally cheaper than a locksmith. A lockout call from a towing company typically runs between $50 and $100 in Las Vegas depending on the time of day and location. Locksmith rates for the same service can run $100 to $150 or more, and after-hours calls push that number higher.

Where a locksmith becomes worth the extra cost is when you need key cutting, key programming for modern transponder keys, or any work on the lock cylinder itself. Those are things a towing company cannot do.

## Which Situation Calls for Which Service

Call a towing company lockout service when your keys are locked inside the car and you just need to get back in. This covers the majority of lockout situations and is the fastest, most affordable option in Las Vegas.

Call a locksmith when you have lost your key entirely and need a replacement cut and programmed, when your lock cylinder is damaged or needs to be re-keyed, or when you need a spare key made.

If you are not sure which situation you are in, call us first. We will tell you honestly whether we can handle it or whether you need a locksmith, and we will not send a truck if we know a locksmith is the right answer.

## A Note on Newer Vehicles

Modern vehicles with smart keys, push-button start, and proximity sensors can be more complicated to deal with in a lockout situation. Some newer cars can be unlocked remotely through a manufacturer app or a dealer's remote assistance line. Before calling anyone, check whether your vehicle has an app — Ford Pass, Toyota Remote Connect, GM's OnStar, and others all offer remote unlock services that cost nothing if you already have the subscription.

If the app does not work or you do not have one, call us and we will let you know if our lockout tools work on your specific vehicle.
