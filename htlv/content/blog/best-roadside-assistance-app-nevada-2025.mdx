The roadside assistance app market has gotten crowded in the last few years. Between the traditional players like AAA, newer on-demand platforms like Urgently and Honk, manufacturer apps, insurance company apps, and credit card benefits, a Nevada driver could spend more time comparing options than they would waiting for a tow truck.

Here is a practical look at the main options available to Nevada drivers in 2025 and how each one actually performs when something goes wrong on a Las Vegas freeway or a remote Nevada highway.

## AAA Mobile App

AAA remains the most established roadside assistance service in Nevada, and their app is the most refined of the traditional membership-based options. The app allows you to request service, share your GPS location with the dispatcher, and track your service vehicle in real time — all without being put on hold.

The key advantage AAA has in Nevada specifically is their network depth. AAA has contracted service providers throughout the state, including coverage in more remote areas like Ely, Winnemucca, and along US-93. If you do a lot of driving across Nevada beyond the Las Vegas-Reno corridor, AAA's statewide coverage matters more than it would for a driver who stays in the valley.

The limitation is the same one we have noted elsewhere: AAA uses contracted providers rather than direct employees, so response times can vary based on who is available in your area at the time you call. The app gives you better visibility into that process but does not change the underlying structure.

The app itself is well-built. Location sharing works reliably, the ETA tracking is reasonably accurate, and you can request most services directly through the app without making a phone call.

## Urgently

Urgently is an on-demand platform that connects drivers with service providers without requiring an annual membership. You pay per service when you need it rather than paying an annual fee in advance.

For Nevada drivers who break down infrequently and want coverage without a subscription, Urgently is worth knowing about. The app works on an Uber-style model — you enter your location and service need, see available providers nearby, get an ETA, and track the vehicle.

The challenge in Las Vegas is the same one all on-demand platforms face in any city: provider availability varies. In the heart of the valley during normal hours, response times are generally reasonable. On a remote highway at 2am, the platform may have limited options. The per-service cost is also higher than the equivalent call through a membership plan if you use it more than a couple of times a year.

## Honk

Honk operates similarly to Urgently — on-demand, no membership required, pay-per-use. The platform has a slightly different contractor network which means availability differs by area and time of day.

For Las Vegas specifically, Honk has decent coverage in the metro area but is thinner in remote Nevada. If your driving is primarily within the valley, it is a viable option. For drivers who travel the state, it is less reliable than AAA.

## Your Insurance Company's App

Most major auto insurers — State Farm, GEICO, Progressive, Allstate, and others — have roadside assistance available either as an included benefit or a low-cost add-on, and most of their apps now allow you to request roadside service directly within the app.

The quality varies significantly by insurer. Some have built genuinely capable in-app request flows with GPS location sharing and provider tracking. Others are essentially a phone number you tap to call their roadside line, with no app-specific functionality.

Check your insurance app specifically. If you have roadside coverage through your policy, find out how to access it through the app before you need it, not during a breakdown.

## Manufacturer Apps

If your vehicle is relatively new, there is a strong chance the manufacturer's app includes roadside assistance. Toyota Remote Connect, Ford Pass, GM's OnStar, Hyundai BlueLink, BMW Connected, and most other manufacturer apps include at least basic roadside coverage for vehicles within their warranty period.

These apps have a significant advantage over third-party platforms in one area: they can communicate directly with your vehicle. If your car has a dead battery, the app already knows your exact location from the car's GPS rather than relying on your phone. If you are locked out and your vehicle has remote unlock capability, the app can unlock the door without sending anyone out at all.

If your car is new enough to have a manufacturer app, check what roadside coverage is included before downloading anything else. You may already have everything you need.

## The Honest Assessment

Every app and platform is only as good as the service provider that shows up. The app is the front end of the experience — what matters is who actually comes to help you and how quickly.

For Nevada drivers who want the most reliable coverage across the state including remote areas, AAA Plus tier remains the strongest overall option. For Las Vegas valley driving specifically, on-demand platforms like Urgently and Honk work adequately for routine situations. Manufacturer apps are underused and worth checking if you have a newer vehicle.
