Las Vegas has a higher concentration of exotic, luxury, and classic vehicles than almost any other city in the country. Between the car culture, the casino crowds, the events like the SEMA show in November, and the number of collectors who call the valley home, you will see more Ferraris, Lamborghinis, and vintage muscle cars on Las Vegas streets on any given weekend than you would in most cities in a month.

When one of those vehicles breaks down or needs to be moved, the standard approach to towing will not work. These cars have specific requirements, and handling them incorrectly causes damage that can cost more to repair than the tow itself.

Here is what you need to know if you own or drive an exotic, luxury, or classic vehicle in Las Vegas.

## Why Standard Towing Does Not Work for These Vehicles

Most passenger vehicles can be towed with a wheel-lift method without serious consequences. Exotic and classic cars are a different situation for several reasons.

Ground clearance is the first issue. Supercars and many sports cars sit extremely low to the ground. The front air dam, front splitter, and undercarriage components of a car like a Ferrari 488 or a Lamborghini Huracan are inches from the pavement. A standard wheel-lift yoke cannot get under these cars without making contact with components that are not designed to take that kind of load.

Drivetrain complexity is the second issue. Many exotic vehicles use all-wheel drive or rear-engine layouts that make wheel-lift towing mechanically problematic. Some rear-engine vehicles cannot be safely towed from the front at all because of how the drivetrain is configured.

Wheel and tire sensitivity is the third issue. Exotic cars often run low-profile tires on large-diameter wheels with significant negative offset. The strap placement used in standard flatbed towing needs to be adjusted to avoid contact with the wheel finish or the sidewall of the tire.

Body panel fragility is the fourth issue. Carbon fiber body panels, wide-body kits, and custom modifications all create attachment points and surfaces that require careful consideration during loading and securing.

## What Proper Exotic Car Towing Requires

A proper tow for an exotic or classic vehicle starts with a flatbed. This is non-negotiable. All four wheels need to be off the ground and the car needs to be transported as a passenger, not dragged on any of its wheels.

Beyond that, the driver needs to understand the specific requirements of your vehicle. That means knowing where the manufacturer-specified lift points are so the car can be winched up the ramp without putting load on components that cannot handle it. It means knowing the correct strap placement to secure the vehicle without scratching wheels or stressing body panels. It means loading the car at the correct angle to clear the front lip.

Soft straps or strap protectors over the wheel straps are standard practice for exotic cars. Wheel net systems that attach to the wheel itself rather than wrapping around the tire are an even better option for very low-profile tires.

For classic cars, the considerations are slightly different. An older vehicle may have a body that flexes more than a modern unibody, and attachment points that were fine when the car was new may be rusted or weakened. A driver experienced with classic vehicles knows to assess this before loading.

## Before You Call Any Tow Company

Ask directly whether the driver has experience with your type of vehicle. A company that regularly tows exotics will know immediately what you are asking and will be able to answer specifically. A company that does not may hedge or give you a vague answer.

Confirm they are sending a flatbed. For any exotic or classic car, a flatbed is the only acceptable tow method.

Mention any specific considerations about your vehicle upfront. Very low front clearance, aftermarket body work, carbon fiber panels, wide body kit, any recent paint or bodywork — tell them before the truck arrives so the driver knows what they are dealing with.

If your vehicle has a manufacturer-specified tow mode or transport mode that raises the suspension or adjusts other settings for towing, know how to activate it before the truck arrives.

## Long Distance and Event Transport

Las Vegas hosts SEMA every November, Barrett-Jackson twice a year, and numerous other automotive events throughout the year that bring exotic and collector vehicles to and from the valley. If you need a vehicle transported to or from Las Vegas for an event, long distance flatbed transport is available.

For these situations, enclosed transport is the premium option that protects the vehicle from road debris and weather during a multi-hour haul. For shorter distances within Nevada and surrounding states, open flatbed is a practical option.
